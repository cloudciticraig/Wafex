The Musson Family

A business that began with a suitcase, a recession and a wildflower.

In 1991, Craig and Michelle Musson left Zimbabwe for Perth. Within months Craig was out of work — laid off from a Swan Valley rose farm on Christmas Eve. What he did next became Wafex.

Three decades on, Wafex is still privately owned by the Musson family, with the next generation — Kerryn and Ryan — carrying the story forward. Family isn't a marketing line here; it's the founding fact of the company.

Our Flowers

Natives, novelties and world classics.

Australian and African wildflowers are our heritage; Ecuadorian roses and Kenyan summer flowers complete the range. Every stem is graded, hydrated and cold-chained from farm to market.

Waxflower

Chamelaucium — Helix™ genetics

The flower that founded Wafex. Our own Helix breeding program, developed with Kings Park Botanic Garden, delivers longer stems, more colours and a season stretched from May to December.

May – Dec

Protea & King Protea

Proteaceae — Ayoba™ series

Sculptural African and Australian-grown proteas, from compact minis to show-stopping kings, sourced from our own farms and long-standing partner growers.

Seed to Vase

One family, the whole chain.

Wafex is one of the few flower companies on earth that is truly vertically integrated — we breed the genetics, grow the crops, and carry every stem to market ourselves.

  1. Breeding

    Helix™ waxflower & boronia genetics, bred with Kings Park Botanic Garden.

  2. Growing

    Our own Australian farms plus 500+ trusted partner growers worldwide.

  3. Exporting

    Australia's leading fresh and dried flower exporter since the 1990s.

  4. Importing

    Roses, summer flowers and novelties from Ecuador, Kenya and beyond.

  5. Wholesale

    Daily supply to florists and wholesalers in every Australian state.

  6. Bouquets & Retail

    Supermarket bouquet production and direct-to-door online flowers.

Thirty-plus years in, the founder is still chasing the perfect flower. That restlessness is why Wafex went from exporting wild-picked waxflower to breeding entirely new varieties now grown on four continents.
